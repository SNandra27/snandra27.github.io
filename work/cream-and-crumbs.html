Cream and Crumbs

A 'customise your own cupcake' ordering website

Step 1: Understand & Empathise

Cupcakes are a popular choice for cakes these days for all occasions. There are many places selling these which is great, but are pre-made and can’t be changed, this is where Cream & Crumbs comes into the picture. This site allows the customer to customise their own cupcakes, so if they have a peculiar choice of sponge flavour and filling combination, it can be done. These orders are then made from scratch, and delivered or collected on a specific date.

Step 2: Research and Analyse

I was able to interview many people around who I know have had some involvement in cupcakes. This involved a working mum who needed some delivered for a kid’s birthday party, to a more formal evening party where they wanted something ‘different’.

My research also involved interviewing a local bakery, and exploring what other cake websites offer. From this research I was able to gather enough information to create a mood board and user personas to help my design really show what this business could offer.

Step 3: Planning & Design

My sitemap for this was a very exciting one as it contained many pages and lots of information. Once I was happy with how I thought all the content would be laid out, I started to create some low fidelity designs using Balsamiq.

The reason why I choice Balsamiq over other software for this task is because I find this software so easy and fun to use. There are many pre-designed features which you can drag and drop into your designs, so instead of spending lots of time on creating i.e. textboxes or social media logos, all this is created and can simply be used with the designs.

Step 4: Implement & Evaluate

As soon as the wireframes were approved, I was able to create high fidelity visuals, which further helped the client to see what their product will look like in the end. Here the client can now see colours, images, and typography. This gave the client a chance to make any last changes, and to approve what they saw before the building prototype stage.
