Personal Progress

This is a website to gain knowledge about a client's life coaching services, and sports and remedial massages. Here you can gain relevant information about the different services provided and costs involved.

This was a freelance project, where I worked on all steps such as research, design, and development. The website was then deployed live and the content changes can now be maintained by the client.

Step 1: Who are the Users? Understanding and Empathising

My client wanted a website to display information regarding his work in Life Coaching and Sports and Remedial Massage. He deals with many customers who come to him in either physical pain or emotionally unstable, sometimes both. My client didn't have much experience in how the website should look in the best way for his customers to see which would make them then want to contact him for a session. Therefore it was left up to me to advise. The current problem was, the users were still quite confused what kind of services were offered, despite them looking on the website, it was quite hard to navigate around, and to find the information, as well as contact details easily.

In order to know my users, I looked into his current customers and discovered my users would be local to the clinic, in a 5 mile radius, and I found the age group to be mainly between 2 groups. Either between 30-40 years, or over 60. Due to the nature of the services, I was also interested in users' lifestyles, which included their jobs. This is because of today's busy work schedules are known to be a big cause in stress and anxiety, 2 of the biggest issues my client helps with.

Step 2: Research and Analysis

I started this phase by interviewing my client which included getting to know his wants and needs. I wanted to know if there was a preference of how he wanted the website to look, what kind of information he to share, and what he wanted to achieve from the website. For this brief I created a responsive product that would be interchangeable between desktop and mobile view in order to fit the clients and users criteria. For this project I wanted to interview users, however, due to confidentiality and the fact that many users are in physical pain, I was only able to access the users through the client. Thus, I could not meet them in person or talk to them directly. As a creative strategy to overcome this, I took a “survey” response, where I sent out a survey to the users that contained a multitude of questions that would help me define the issues they needed solving and how to best design them a seamless experience using the product. I asked questions such as; Would they like to see prices of the services offered online? Would they prefer to read about the coach before committing to an appointment. What's their preference when making contact, call or email?

Other research including investigating 'Osteopath' and 'Counselling' type websites, to see their designs, and the way information was displayed. I also did a thorough research in colour psychology, typography, and images for this particular type of website. Using my findings I always feel creating a moodboard helps me decide on the designs. Once I felt I had enough information about the customers needs gathered, I created my user personas.

Step 3: Persona, Planning and Design

I collaborated all the information I collected from my research and user profiles to understand my audience and their needs. From this I was able to map out a sitemap, at first a very rough one to see which information goes where, keeping in mind the information needed to be easy to find, as the nature of the website, clients would be using this when possibly stressed or in pain.

Once the sitemap was in place, I used Balsamiq to create wireframes. This allowed me further imagine where everything would go, and I was able to show my client the design ideas, without having to dwell too much on features like colours, typography, and images. For now, I wanted my client to be happy with what he would see layout wise.

I created a quick prototype in Axure for my client to see how the menu would work, and what buttons would lead where. This allowed my client to get a feel of how his website would work as well as more idea of what it would look like as I added in colours and links. After using the prototype, my client felt more confident in knowing what he would get, and we were able to proceed with the website.

Step 4: Implement & Evaluate

As with all projects, feedback went back and forth, and we worked together to create the best possible website for my client. We wanted the website to look very minimal, with calming colours and clear typography. I decided on the images and colours after the research of other similar websites, where pastel colours and no dark backgrounds created a more calming effect. It was important the website was responsive as we wanted customers to be able to view on their phones and for it to look presentable.

The information has been kept very basic and to the point as I found in my research people looking for this kind of information dont want to spend a lot of time reading about it, they would like to know enough to understand this is the right direction, and then would prefer to pick up the phone to ask for more detail or just book an appointment and speak in person, again I feel this is because of the nature of the website.

I recommended to my client to add Google Analytics to the website, so we could track how many people visit his website. As he has many social media accounts, this would make the findings more interesting to see where his advertising really work for him. This would also help us to see how people used his website. My client has noticed an increase in the number of people who reach out to him via email. This shows many people find it more confortable to make contact without having to speak to someone. He has also noticed a difference in the questions his potential customers ask him, already understanding the basic information, and looking for some further details.
