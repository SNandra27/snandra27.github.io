Personal Progress

A website to gain knowledge about life coaching services, and sports massages. This was a freelance project, where I managed the research, and low and high fidelity designs.

As well as hand sketches, I used Balsamiq for the low fidelity wireframes, and Axure for the high fidelity wireframes and prototypes. Photoshop was mainly used for any imagery work.

The Problem

A website was needed to display information regarding the services offered in Life Coaching and Sports Massages. The problem my client had was he felt the number of further enquiries he was receiving was much lower considering the high volume of visitors on his website. He wanted to improve these numbers which would result in potential customers feeling more confident in him after exploring his website to then want to contact him for an appointment.

Research - Surveys

  • I interviewed my client to find out what problems he was having with his current website and what his end goal was.

  • After discussing this, together we decided we would create a responsive website that would be interchangeable between desktop and mobile view in order to fit the clients and users criteria.

  • His customers preferred to remain anonymous thus, I could not meet them in person or talk to them directly. As a creative strategy to overcome this, I took a “survey” response, where I left surveys for the users to fill at their appointments.

    • Survey - The Results

  • My users would be local to the clinic, in a 5 mile radius.

  • The age group to be mainly between 30-60 years.

  • Due to the nature of the services, I was also interested in users' lifestyles, which included their jobs. This is because of today's busy work schedules are known to be a big cause in stress and anxiety, two of the biggest issues my client helps with. Using this information helped me create some user personas.

    • Moodboard

  • For market research, I analysed other websites offering similar services to observe what information was shared and the experience these websites gave.

  • Part of the research was to also look at the type of images used, typography, font sizes, common colours to name a few. This allowed me to see what calls out to users similar to mine and what is usable and functional for them.

  • Once this was done, I always found creating a moodboard at this stage helped me gather my thoughts around colours, images, and font types.

    • Planning and Design - Sitemap

  • I collaborated all the information I collected from my research and user profiles to understand my audience and their needs.

  • From this I was able to create a sitemap, to organise the hierachy of the website and to see which information will go where.

  • The information needed to be easy to find, as the nature of the website, clients would be using this when possibly stressed or in pain.

    • Planning and Design - Wireframes

    Once the sitemap was in place, I used Balsamiq to create wireframes. This allowed me to further imagine where everything would go, and I was able to show my client the design ideas, without having to dwell too much on features like colours, typography, and images. For now, I wanted my client to be happy with what he would see layout wise.

    Planning and Design - Prototype

    I created a quick prototype in Axure for my client to see how the menu would work, and what buttons would lead where. This allowed my client to get a feel of how his website would work as well as more idea of what it would look like as I added in colours and links. After using the prototype, my client felt more confident in knowing what he would get, and we were able to proceed with the website.

    Implement & Evaluate

    As with all projects, feedback went back and forth, and we worked together to create the best possible website for my client. We wanted the website to look very minimal, with calming colours and clear typography. I decided on the images and colours after the research of other similar websites, where pastel colours and no dark backgrounds created a more calming effect. It was important the website was responsive as we wanted customers to be able to view on their phones and for it to look presentable.

    The information has been kept very basic and to the point as I found in my research people looking for this kind of information dont want to spend a lot of time reading about it, they would like to know enough to understand this is the right direction, and then would prefer to pick up the phone to ask for more detail or just book an appointment and speak in person, again I feel this is because of the nature of the website.

    I recommended to my client to add Google Analytics to the website, so we could track how many people visit his website. As he has many social media accounts, this would make the findings more interesting to see where his advertising really work for him. This would also help us to see how people used his website. My client has noticed an increase in the number of people who reach out to him via email. This shows many people find it more confortable to make contact without having to speak to someone. He has also noticed a difference in the questions his potential customers asked him, clearly showing they are finding answers on the website.
